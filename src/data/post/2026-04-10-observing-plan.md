This kind of entry works well for weekly observing preparation and short-term adjustments. The point is not to sound polished.
The point is to preserve the decisions you are most likely to forget later.

## Priorities for this week

- Confirm the highest-priority targets and the observing windows associated with each of them.
- Record weather, moon phase, altitude, and instrument availability constraints.
- Write down why any target was dropped or rescheduled so that the decision still makes sense a few days later.

## Information worth keeping

- The target list and selection criteria.
- Changes in the observing schedule.
- Whether calibration data are complete.
- The questions most worth revisiting this week.

## Next step

If you keep using this site consistently, a short weekly observing post can gradually become a very clear timeline of your research process.
