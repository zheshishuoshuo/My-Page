Paper notes are often most useful when they are short. Instead of writing a long summary, keep the information you are most likely to revisit.

## What is most worth recording

- What question the paper is actually trying to answer.
- Whether its data, sample, or method are close to your own work.
- Which figure or table is most worth coming back to.
- What important problem the paper still leaves open.

## A compact structure

### Question

What phenomenon is the paper trying to explain, or what hypothesis is it testing?

### Method

What data, models, fitting steps, or observing strategies are being used?

### Result

Which conclusions feel most important, and which ones should be treated carefully?

### Relevance to my work

Connect the paper directly to your own project. This is often the most valuable part of the note.
